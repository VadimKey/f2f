A Classical Liberal Framework for Analyzing Political Systems

Core Definition

Fascism = Any regime with high state coercion that prevents people from freely choosing or changing their political system, regardless of whether it calls itself communist, monarchist, nationalist, or fascist.

Power structures matter more than ideology. What matters is whether individuals can peacefully change their system and have protected negative rights (freedom FROM coercion).

The Test: Can People Freely Choose and Change Their Political System?

  • YES ‚Üí Not fascist (even with collective ownership or welfare states)
  • NO ‚Üí Fascist (regardless of economic ideology)

Freedom-Coercion Spectrum

  • Most Free: Strong constitutional protections, rule of law, minimal coercion, easy exit
  • Very Free: Functioning democracy with robust rights but some bureaucratic burden
  • Free: Democratic system with growing concerns about rights erosion
  • Moderately Free: Democracy under stress with significant institutional weaknesses
  • Moderately Fascist: Controlled elections, systematic coercion, difficult exit
  • Highly Fascist: No meaningful choice, cannot change system, exit blocked

Philosophical Basis

Classical liberalism (Locke, Mill, Hayek, Popper, Nozick, Berlin) - "one step short of anarchism" - minimal state constrained by constitutional protection of negative rights.
